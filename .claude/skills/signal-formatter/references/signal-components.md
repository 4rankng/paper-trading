# Signal Components Explained

## Action
The trading decision: BUY, SELL, HOLD, or TRIM

## Driver Class
Which market machine is driving this decision:
- **HYPE_MACHINE:** Sentiment-driven, short-term trading
- **EARNINGS_MACHINE:** Fundamental-driven, long-term investing
- **MEAN_REVERSION_MACHINE:** Statistical arbitrage on Graham gaps

## Levels
Price targets and limits:
- **Entry:** Trigger price or current market price
- **Exit:** Target price or valuation-based exit
- **Stop:** Maximum loss threshold (asymmetric by driver class)

## Meta
Position metadata:
- **Size:** Dollar amount or % of portfolio
- **Confidence:** HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW (or 1-10 scale)
- **Timeframe:** Expected holding period (months or years)

## Risks
Top 3 SPECIFIC risks (not generic):
- ❌ "Market risk" (too generic)
- ✅ "Fed hikes rates, killing growth multiple" (specific)
- ✅ "Competitor launches v2.0 making tech obsolete" (specific)
- ✅ "Liquidity dries up, can't exit without slippage" (specific)

## Opportunity Cost
What you're displacing:
- SPY (S&P 500 index)
- QQQ (Nasdaq-100 index)
- Another ticker you're not buying
- Expected alpha over the alternative

## Machine Logic
Why this driver class applies:
- Evidence for this classification
- Why NOT the other machine types (explicit falsification)

## Benchmark Comparison
Comparison vs SPY/QQQ with:
- 6M returns
- Trend analysis
- Volatility metrics
- Expected alpha
- Recommendation (BUY STOCK vs BUY INDEX)
